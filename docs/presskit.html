Description


Death Pilot it's a FPS that blends the disturbances cosmic horror and the extremeness of psychological horror to accentuate the paradigms unfold by its premise. Infested with horrifying creatures lurking in every corner, chilling enigmas that obstruct your progress and a unsettle story that challenges the implications that define us as humans. Visually inspired by cult horror classics which permeate our sidereal imagination and accompanied musically by pieces that immerse us in the depths of our dichotomous internal worlds and their latent fears.


Alexandro, a clone with the only objective to terraform Reba, a desolate, hostile planet reduced to rubble after a terrible war against the Rebanarians. He doesn’t know what or who he is but, with the help of Roxana, an artificial intelligence attached to his suit that manipulates him, they will cross the confines of what was once the planet, looking for supplies and clues while being stalked by catastrophic weather conditions and dangerous figures. Among them are the Teratogens, current rulers of Reba, and biological weapons created hundreds of years ago by humans, parasites capable of linking through a Hive mind and characterized by their peculiar predilection for human flesh. Alex's past, Roxana's secrets, and the mysteries surrounding Reba rises as he descends into Reba's entrails toward the Hive, where the origin of his nightmares hides. However, by interacting with each of these agents, he will question whether Alex should trust the judgment of the wanderers and fulfill his desires or be guided by his instincts and decide between sacrificing himself and saving the planet or being consumed by madness and taking everything to hell with him.


Features


  • Tense First-Person Survival: Every step could be your last. Conserve your resources, make every shot count, and navigate a hostile environment where danger lurks in every shadow.
  • Strategic Battery Management: Your life depends on energy — literally. Collect and distribute power across your battery modules to keep crucial systems online. Choose wisely; running out of energy might mean sealing your fate.
  • Immersive, Oppressive Atmosphere: Dive into a world drenched in tension and mystery. The environment reacts subtly to your presence, building an eerie sense of unease as you unravel its dark secrets.
  • Resourceful Exploration: Survival isn’t just about shooting — it’s about managing what little you have. Scour every corner for precious energy, hidden tools, and clues that could mean the difference between escape and oblivion.
  • Unravel a Cryptic Narrative: The story won’t reveal itself easily. Piece together fragmented clues scattered across a desolate world and uncover the truth behind the shadows.
  • A World of Unfettered Imagination: Death Pilot draws from the essence of classic survival horror while crafting a wholly original universe. Every decision, every moment, shapes your fate.

About Hollow Sense

The Hollow Sense team met in early 2020 before the pandemic. Alberto Ramirez (CEO) trained the programming team (Robert and Emmanuel) in Unreal Engine for a year, while Perla was in charge of the 3D area. At the end of that year, the idea of ​​creating a video game with a cosmic and psychological horror theme began to be conceptualized, to the point that the first approaches to the initial phases of Death Pilot began to be outlined. After a year, the team continued to delve into the world of Game Dev until they reunited and decided on March 14, 2023, to focus on the foundations and the concept of what that crazy idea of ​​a first-person survival horror could be.


After a year and a half of pauses and continuous training in video game development and art techniques, they decided to start the Death Pilot project with the name the team had agreed upon: Hollow Sense.


The focus of Hollow Sense is not on representing specific cultural elements of Mexico, but on demonstrating the country's creativity in building imaginary worlds and also on training by the crew in Unreal Engine 5 for all people interested in learning the world of game dev from a friendly and continuous perspective by its main members with tools and courses that they offer to students and assistants of the Death Pilot project.
